What is our Product?

Our product is called Clean Sneaks, and its an automatic shoe cleaning device. The idea came to me when I was thinking about things that would make my life easier, and one thing was always buying new shoes instead of saving my dirty shoes. This IOT device will be compatible with some of the most popular shoe models including Jordans, Dr. Marten's, Yeezys, and various other types of shoes.

Laws of UI Design example - Jakob's Law


One small example of Jakob's Law can be seen in the interface of old car radios and newer car entertainment centers. Back when I had a 2008 Toyota Corolla, I had to endure only having a radio & CD interface, no auxilary cable or bluetooth connectivity. This radio's interface is pictured on the left. I found myself getting use to the radio interface over the years, and learned that the long press on one of the numbered buttons would set that station into the number's "memory". Once I got a newer car, I had to learn the new entertainment interface, to my pleasure, I found that setting the station into memory was the same action, the long press.
Laws of UI Design example - Doherty Threshold


An example of Doherty Threshold can be seen within the gaming community, and can especially be seen in the handheld emulator market as well as online gameplay. When a gamer notices that his/her framerate is getting choppy, they will lose interest in playing that game. A smooth gameplay experience is very important in the gaming community, and without it you will lose users.
